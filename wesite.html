Reflection on our first PESE-300 class

15 June, 2025 - Tuesday

During our latest PESE 300 class, I realized that this subject teaches us to enhance our employability skills by doing activities hands-on. These skills blend technical expertise, functional skills, and appropriate attitude - all of which make one useful in a business or work environment. An individual can be very competent in his or her area of expertise but lack a good employability skill such that they could not effectively communicate or showcase their skills. That's why it's important not only to study but also to cultivate the ability to showcase and implement knowledge in the proper manner. In the Q&A session, I realized that actually not knowing an answer is not the problem - it's evading the question. Rather, we ought to always seek to comprehend the 'why' in things, which lies at the core of engineering mentality. Technical skills are necessary, but it's also crucial to have a record of what you do, say, on GitHub, so recruiters and HR specialists can view your projects and skills firsthand. Later, during the practical exercise when we were introducing ourselves.

  • Employability skills are a blend of technical expertise, functional skills, and attitude.
  • Always seek to understand the 'why' behind things.
  • Maintain a record of your work on platforms like GitHub.
  • Confidence and enthusiasm in self-introduction can leave a lasting impression.
  • Never underestimate yourself; your attitude matters as much as your skills.

SWOC Analysis

22 June, 2025 - Tuesday

We started by going through the definition and function of PESE. Then we began Unit 1 on SWOC - Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Challenges. Strengths: the things we do naturally well and possess skills in. Weaknesses: things where others might be better at them than us. So as to understand these better, we had a rapid-fire round where each of us quickly mentioned their strengths and weaknesses. This helped us see that: Strengths and weaknesses are internal attributes. For example, family in itself isn't a strength, but their support can assist one in developing one. We also discussed Opportunities and Challenges by dividing ourselves into a group of seven students. Each group brainstormed ideas and then presented them to the class. Opportunities: good external factors like studying at Graphic Era, internships, and learning new platforms. Challenges: external challenges like rapidly changing technology, environmental issues, or economic downturns.

SWOC Analysis

29 June, 2025 - Tuesday

In this session, our group - Piyush, Aditya, Aman, Deepanshu, Gaurav and Me - analyzed the SWOC of a student from Jhumri Telaiya who aimed to study BTech in AI/ML. Strengths - Curiosity and eagerness to learn - many village students have a strong drive to prove themselves, which is a huge advantage in tech. - Strong academic foundation - traditional schooling often builds solid math skills, which are crucial for AI/ML. - Adaptability - learning with limited resources develops resilience, which is an essential skill in the fast-changing tech environment. Weaknesses - Access to good resources like internet speed, laptops, or guides is limited. - Low exposure to AI/ML and related career prospects without the right guidance. - Difficulty in communication due to a lack of language proficiency, as most technical materials are in English. Opportunities - Free online platforms like Coursera, YouTube, Kaggle, and ChatGPT are available. - Government scholarships like NSP and AICTE. - Rising work-from-home culture in technology, allowing students to work for top companies without having to move. Challenges - Limited budgets for courses, equipment, or traveling. - Family pressures nudging towards conventional career streams, making AI/ML look risky and unconventional.
